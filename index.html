Solar System

The solar system is a group of space objects that orbit around the Sun, which is a big, hot star at the center. The Sun’s gravity holds everything together, like a giant magnet in space. There are eight main planets that orbit the Sun: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. The first four planets are rocky, and the last four are gas or ice giants. Earth is the third planet and the only one known to have life. In addition to planets, the solar system has moons, asteroids, comets, and dwarf planets like Pluto. Moons orbit planets, and some planets have dozens of them — Jupiter has the most! Between Mars and Jupiter is the asteroid belt, filled with rocky objects. Far beyond Neptune is a cold region called the Kuiper Belt, where many icy objects and dwarf planets live. The solar system is over 4.5 billion years old. It’s huge, and it takes light from the Sun about 8 minutes to reach Earth. Our solar system is just a small part of a galaxy called the Milky Way, but it’s our cosmic home — full of wonder and mystery!

Sun: The Star at the Center

The Sun is a giant ball of hot, glowing gas. It might look small in the sky, but it’s actually huge—so big that more than one million Earths could fit inside it! The Sun is the center of our solar system, and all the planets, including Earth, go around it. Without the Sun, there would be no light, no warmth, and no life on Earth. The Sun is a star, just like the ones you see twinkling in the night sky. But it looks much bigger and brighter because it’s the closest star to us. The Sun is about 93 million miles (150 million kilometers) away from Earth. Even though that’s very far, its light only takes about 8 minutes to reach us! Read more....

Earth: Our Home Planet

Earth is a very special planet. It’s the only one we know of where people, animals, and plants can live. That’s why we call it our “home planet.” Earth is the third planet from the Sun, and it’s just the right distance to not be too hot or too cold. It’s like the “Goldilocks” planet—not too big, not too small, but just right! One of the coolest things about Earth is that it has land, air, and water. About 70% of Earth’s surface is covered with water—oceans, lakes, and rivers. That’s why Earth looks mostly blue when you see pictures of it from space. The land gives us places to live, grow food, and explore. The air is full of oxygen, which people and animals need to breathe. All of these things work together to make life possible. Read more....

Moon: Earth's Space Buddy

The Moon is like Earth’s best friend in space! It’s the only natural satellite of our planet, which means it orbits around Earth just like how Earth orbits the Sun. You can often spot it shining brightly in the night sky, and sometimes it even looks like it's smiling down at us. The Moon is not a planet; it's much smaller than Earth. It’s about one-quarter the size of our planet, and if you could fit Earth inside the Moon, it would fit about four moons inside! Even though it’s smaller, the Moon has a big impact on Earth.
